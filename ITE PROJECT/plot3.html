Plot 3 – Crop Diversity & Sustainability

Snehal Farms

About Plot 3

Plot 3 represents the most active and versatile agricultural land of Snehal Farms. This plot reflects the farm’s present-day focus on crop diversity, seasonal planning, and sustainable farming practices.

Unlike earlier plots that focused on specific crops, Plot 3 is designed to support multiple crops throughout the year based on soil condition, water availability, and market demand.

Crops Grown in Plot 3

Plot 3 is currently used for cultivating a variety of crops, which helps in maintaining soil fertility and reducing crop-related risks.

  • Soyabean – Improves soil health and supports crop rotation.
  • Sugarcane – Grown using efficient irrigation practices.
  • Onion – Cultivated with careful water and nutrient management.
  • Wheat – A seasonal crop supporting food grain production.
  • Other crops – Depending on season and land conditions.

This mixed cropping approach ensures better land utilization and stable production throughout the year.

Farming Approach

Plot 3 follows a balanced farming approach that combines traditional knowledge with modern agricultural techniques. Crop rotation is practiced to preserve soil nutrients and prevent pest buildup.

Efficient irrigation methods and timely field preparation help improve crop yield while minimizing water usage.

Future Vision of Plot 3

Plot 3 plays a key role in the future plans of Snehal Farms. The focus remains on sustainable agriculture, improved productivity, and responsible use of natural resources.

With continuous learning and adaptation, this plot will continue to support multiple crops while meeting the growing agricultural challenges of the future.
